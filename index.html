Concrete cross-boundary failure demo

One filing record is tracked from source data to validation. The point is not that users literally make one cartoonish mistake, but that realistic ETL, calendarization, join, and panel-construction steps can gradually strip away the time constraint that made the record legal.

Shared record used in every step: ticker=A, period_end=2024-03-31, filed_at=2024-05-10, available_time=2024-05-10, decision_time=2024-04-01, label_window=[2024-04-02, 2024-04-08], eps_surprise=+0.18.
The concrete failure pattern is this: a quarterly filing row is eventually expanded into a daily modeling panel. The local tools can all be correct, yet the daily expansion can still make the feature appear usable on 2024-04-01 even though the record only becomes legal on 2024-05-10.
